Course Documents and Next Steps

The following is a brief list of important documents you will find in this course. After reading it carefully, you will find next steps below.

Course Documents

Course Outline

This is an official document generated by the college that outlines the course and clearly lists the objectives which will be covered within the course.

Assignment Due Dates and Grading Rubrics

This document provides a quick summary of due dates and values of assessments.

Weekly Course Schedule

This document breaks down the weekly course schedule.

Questions or Comments Forum

This is a general discussion area where students can post questions and respond to other students. The facilitator will oversee this area and respond as required.

Announcements

Important information and weekly updates will be posted as needed by the facilitator, check regularly.

General Discussions

This is a general forum for you, the learners, to post news, items of general interest, conduct side discussions and so on. Please post any messages of a social nature here.

Next Steps

Now that you are familiar with the course layout, documents, supportive resources, policies, and your facilitator you are ready to start exploring the other areas of this course. The following guidance will help you get started.

  1. Read through the Course Outline and Course Overview and Schedule modules
    Download and print the Course Outline. Everything you need to do for this course is listed on this schedule. Also print out the Course Overview and Schedule document for your reference.
  2. Update your Profile with a valid email address
    When your D2L account was created, you may have been assigned a generic email address of ontarioLearn@embanet.com. This information must be updated on D2L with a valid personal email address if you want to receive direct communication from your facilitator and other participants. If you do not have an email account, you can create one on the Gmail or Yahoo sites.
    • In D2L, you will see the following are at the top right of your screen:
    • Click on your name, and then select Profile
    • In the email field delete ontariolearn@embanet.com and enter YOUR personal email address. Check to make sure that the information you have entered is correct.
    • Click to save the change.
  3. Introduce yourself to your peers and facilitator
    Get to know your course colleagues by posting your introduction under Student Introductions. Instructions for this posting are provided in the forum.
